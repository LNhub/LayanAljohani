The Critical Gap

Why ETM?

When natural disasters or political crises strike abroad, every second counts. Currently, there is no centralized, automated system in Saudi Arabia specifically designed to instantaneously identify, prioritize, and evacuate citizens located in danger zones. The process often relies on manual communication, which can be slow and prone to delays.

My Vision: I proposed ETM (Etmeanan) as a definitive solution. A system where the citizen takes zero action. The system automatically detects their location via Absher, checks their medical status for priority, books their evacuation ticket, and notifies them instantly.

The Academic Evolution

This project wasn't built in a day. It is the result of deep technical layering across three major university courses, evolving from a theoretical concept to a fully architected database system.

Phase 1: Inception

Course: System Analysis & Design

Here, the idea of "Reassurance" was born. I conducted feasibility studies, defined the problem scope, and identified key stakeholders (Absher, Airlines, Embassies). The focus was on gathering functional requirements to solve the evacuation latency problem.

Phase 2: Modeling

Course: Modeling & Simulation

I took the concept and gave it structure. Using UML standards, I designed the system's behavior. I created Sequence Diagrams to map the data flow between the Citizen and the Booking Manager, and Activity Diagrams to visualize the automated decision-making process.

Phase 3: Architecture

Course: Database Systems

The final and most complex layer. I transformed the models into a robust Relational Database. I designed the ER Diagram, established Entity constraints, and wrote SQL logic to handle the "Illness Flag" priority system and M:N relationships between transport companies.
